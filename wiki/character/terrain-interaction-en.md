# Character - Terrain Interaction

---

## Definition

- General cover and concealment do NOT count as terrain interaction
- Terrain interaction occurs when a specific action is performed at a designated location (typically exploiting terrain features)
- Pre-placed interaction triggers on terrain → interaction begins when the player contacts the trigger and executes the action

---

## Terrain Interaction Types

### Climb

- Action for scaling terrain that cannot be jumped over but can be pulled up onto with hand movements
- Chest height up to a maximum of ___cm
- To prevent climbing in unintended locations, **climb volumes are placed only at designated spots**
- Climbing is only possible at those locations using the dedicated interaction button

| Perspective | Motion |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st person | Weapon is put away; both arms reach forward into view, then sweep down toward the chest while pulling up |
| 3rd person | Wall climb (pulling up onto the surface, not vaulting over) |

### Vault

- Action for jumping over relatively low obstacles to the other side
- Faster and smoother than a regular jump (parkour style)
- Climb = pulling up onto a higher surface / Vault = jumping over a flat obstacle

| Perspective | Motion |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st person | Left hand reaches out to push off the obstacle; the vault is conveyed through camera movement |
| 3rd person | Left hand plants on the obstacle and the body swings over |

### Ladder Hang

- **Grabbing a vertical ladder**: Can grab directly from flat ground or from the middle of a ladder
- **Top end of ladder**: Body lowers down and transitions into a hanging motion
- **Middle of Ladder Volume**: Grab onto the ladder
- **Top of Ladder Volume**: Mount the top / Switch to hanging from the top
- The first rung of every ladder must be unified at **30cm intervals**

| Perspective | Motion |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st person | Climbing up or descending the ladder |
| 3rd person | Climbing up or descending the ladder |

> A single motion is used — played forward, paused, or played in reverse to represent ascending and descending

### Slide Slope

- Interacting at the top causes the character to slide down to the bottom
- Slope angle is unified at **45 degrees** for all slides
- Interaction from the bottom going up is not possible

| Perspective | Motion |
|-------------|--------|
| 1st person | Automatic movement with no dedicated motion (shooting and aiming are possible) |
| 3rd person | Only the lower body is shown crouched down |
