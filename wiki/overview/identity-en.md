# Game One-Line Definition & Identity

Defines the core identity and market positioning of a PvPvE vehicle extraction shooter that layers Battlefield's armored vehicle combat texture onto an extraction loop.

---

## 1-1. One-Line Definition

**WarRock 2: Mavericks** -- A PvPvE vehicle extraction shooter where a 4-person mercenary squad engages in Battlefield-style vehicle+infantry hybrid combat across a chaotic post-civil-war desert in Derbaran, recovering contract cargo and escaping via Chinook.

> Armored vehicle combat is this game's "unique core" and a non-negotiable premise. Infantry extraction has many references, but as of 2026, no extraction game exists where vehicles serve as the main inventory and protagonist.

---

## 1-2. Core Identity

This game's identity is **"a game where Battlefield's armored vehicle combat texture is transposed onto an extraction loop."** The infantry full-stack custom meta (Tarkov, Hunt: Showdown, Dark and Darker) is already a saturated red ocean, and WR2 does not ride that track. Instead, it layers Battlefield's combat texture -- the rhythm of alternating between mounted vehicle combat and dismounted infantry combat, tank+Humvee+truck composite formations, engineer mine warfare, dismounted infiltration -- onto extraction's contract/extraction/loss tension structure.

> Armored vehicle combat is this game's "unique core" and a non-negotiable premise. Infantry extraction has many references, but as of 2026, no extraction game exists where vehicles serve as the main inventory and protagonist. Seizing this gap is the project's reason for existence.

**What we take from Battlefield**: Desert vehicle squad combat with tank+Humvee+truck composite formations, alternating between mounted and dismounted play, anti-vehicle counterplay including engineer mines/AT rockets/RPGs, terrain design (undulating desert with rocks/canyons/cliffs, not flat plains).

**What we take from extraction**: Contract-based farming structure (Tarkov dealer system), extraction/loss tension (we discard the pre-combat preparation meta), bot/player mixing (Hunt: Showdown), Chinook beacon climax.

**What we don't take**: Tarkov-style gear Tetris/individual infantry full-stack customization, Sea of Thieves' no-real-loss structure, Hazard Zone's structure where combat replaces objectives.

## 1-4. Positioning

| Existing Extraction Games | WR2: Mavericks |
|---------------------------|----------------|
| Infantry is the protagonist | Vehicles are the protagonist; infantry is a tool for town operations |
| Gear Tetris / full-stack customization | Monolithic class swapping (Battlefield-style) |
| Solo survival | 4-person convoy management |
| Escape = open a door / helicopter procedure | Escape = truck survival + 90-second Chinook holdout climax |
| Individual farming | Squad contract cargo + truck cargo bed progression |

---

## 1-5. Critical Difference from Battlefield Hazard Zone

Hazard Zone had a structure where combat could replace objectives. Killing other teams and stealing their drives was sufficient, which led to the service shutting down within weeks of launch. In WR2, escaping without a truck limits you to small cargo only, there is no Chinook climax, and long-term progression is significantly slower by design -- ensuring that contract success always maintains the highest value. Drop rates of 40-60% and decay rates of 30-55% are balance points designed to limit steal advantages while keeping farming a more stable income source than combat.
