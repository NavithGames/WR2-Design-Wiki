# Ammunition Types

---

## Pistol Rounds
Ammunition for pistols and submachine guns

- **Level-N FMJ**: A round with penetration rating capable of piercing Level-N armor. For each level the bullet's penetration rating falls below the armor's protection level, an n% damage reduction effect applies
- **Hollow Point (HP)**: Generally has lower penetration rating but inflicts greater knockback on the target. Strong knockback breaks ADS and causes muzzle shake, making counterfire difficult. High-tier HP rounds also have Level-n penetration capability

---

## Rifle Rounds
Ammunition for assault rifles and rifles

- **FMJ**: Level-n penetration
- **Armor-Piercing (AP)**: Very high penetration rating, can damage vehicles, but reduces rate of fire

---

## Sniper Rounds
Large-caliber rounds exclusive to sniper rifles and DMRs

- **FMJ**: Standard sniper round
- **Armor-Piercing (AP)**: High penetration
- **Subsonic**: Reduced muzzle velocity. When combined with a suppressor, noise reduction approaches assault rifle suppressor levels. Significantly reduced effective range

---

## Shotgun Shells

- **Cluster (Buckshot)**: Number of simultaneous projectiles varies from 3-12 depending on gauge value. Higher gauge means more projectiles
- **Slug**: Projectile count reduced to 1, but range and grouping approach rifle-round levels
